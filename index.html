IST-264 Prototyping and Usability Project

Redesigning a Planner app

Our Team

Mercury Bao and Danielle Smith

Who's the Customer?

People who use a planner like students, teachers, or those who like to maintain organization in their lives.

The Problem

  • There are all kinds of users with different habits, so we'll have satisfy a majority of them but unfortunately won't be able to get all of them.

  • The two main types of people we can classify being exposed to this app are people who love to plan and people who hate to plan.

  • Another pain point is that there are too many planner apps so we'll have to make ours stand out from the rest of them.

  • People often find planner apps annoying or forget to use them so we'll need to come up with a way to keep users engaged with our app and keep using it to help organize their lives.

  • Not all planer apps are built the same, they each have their own advantages and disadvantages, so ideally it would be best to take some of the best features of them and include them in our app.

Our Analysis

  • One aspect of this prototype is to be able to show how to create new events and tasks for your schedule and how to modify or delete existing ones.

  • Another aspect of this prototype is showing how to create tasks and the ability to check them off when you complete them.

  • The overall goal is to help keep people organized if they decide to use this app and like it as opposed to using something else.

Evaluation of our lo-fi prototypes

We tested a few people we knew on our lo-fi prototpye to see what they thought of it. Overall, it seems like that our prototype needs some work in terms of interaction and making things less cluttered and more simplified on the page.

The feedback that I got from the two participants that I tested specifically had somewhat similar feedback though worded differently. I had them both look at our desktop version first and then the mobile version and allowed them to explore what was going on for a few minutes on their own without any prompt. Afterwards, I then asked them what were their thoughts on both versions and encourage to make suggestions on how they would like to see features layed out and took notes on and documented them.

Both of these participants are familiar with tehcnology and it was interesting to see that one participant preferred the desktop version while the other one preferred the mobile version. I thought that the feedback we got from our participants was valueable since it allows us to see where our flaws are and start to think of ideas of how to fix them when we make our hi-fi prototype. I definitely think that once we apply these suggestions, it will feel more like a version that can be used rather than some broken screens which is what they feel like at the moment due to no interaction between them for both versions.

Evaluation of our Hi-fi Prototype

Participants were selected with the convenience sampling, which means that experimenter chose the people based on their availability at the moment. They were informed that they can leave the experiment at any time if they want to, and they can choose to continue with the experiment if they want to. They are also told that the experiment is for testing prototype, not them.

We selected 4 participants, and the task given is:

  • Imagine if this is a portion of planner app you have been using for a while. Here are three tasks that I ask you to complete:
  • Can you try to check what is due this week?
  • Can you go back to the home page and check off one task? Can you uncheck it?
  • Can you create a new task?

The functionality tested are: scrolling through list of tasks, checking and unchecking a task, creating a new task.

All of our participants pointed out things in our prototype that could use some improvement due to either not making sense in their current form or not being there at all. It seemed that the mobile version was preferred over the desktop version. A couple of our participants have seen our project before when they viewed the lo-fi screens and they did point out that because some usability was added, there was improvement in that area.

Our next step with this project is to:

  • Create a more distinguished difference between the desktop version and the mobile version since it was pointed out that they look very similar
  • Allow for customization of different levels of tasks (like color tags for different levels of urgency)
  • Make sure all interactions make sense in terms of where they take you from point A to point B
  • Do another round of testing once we take what was suggested to us and try to implement them into our project
